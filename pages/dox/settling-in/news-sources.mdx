## Newspapers
Dutch newspapers are issued every day in the Netherlands, except Sunday and some public holidays. The three most widely-read national newspapers in the Netherlands are [De Telegraaf](https://www.telegraaf.nl/), [Algemeen Dagblad](https://www.ad.nl/), and [Metro](https://www.metronieuws.nl/). Keep in mind that Dutch newspapers are only in Dutch. However, you can use Google translate or reading Metro is a good way to supplement your Dutch if you are interested. 

There are also regional newspapers in the Netherlands like [Het Parool](https://www.parool.nl/?referrer=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.google.com%2F). These provide important information about what is going on in your region. 

## News websites 
You can find both Dutch and English news websites in the Netherlands. Although there are many websites provide news and information in Dutch, [NOS.nl](https://nos.nl/ ) is the most popular one among Dutch people. Moreover, English-speaking expats living in the Netherlands can access news in English from a variety of websites, including the following:

-[Dutch News](https://www.dutchnews.nl/) is the leading provider of quality Dutch news in English for an international audience. The website covers topics such as news, business, sport, and entertainment. Readers can also subscribe to receive daily digital newsletters.

-[NL Times](https://nltimes.nl/) is the most regularly updated English news website in the Netherlands. It regularly publishes Dutch news in English, with sections such as crime, politics, business, sports, and technology.

-[Expatica](https://www.expatica.com/nl/) publishes regular news updates in English on its website in the Netherlands; covering topics that affect expats living.

-[Government.nl](https://www.government.nl/) provides up-to-date news reports in English from the Dutch government about issues affecting the country.
